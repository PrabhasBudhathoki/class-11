chpater-8:Information Security and Cyber Law

Chapter 8, Information Security and Cyber Law, focuses on the critical areas of protecting digital information and understanding the legal frameworks that govern its use in the digital world. The chapter introduces the concept of information security, which is the practice of safeguarding digital data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction. It covers various threats to information security, such as viruses, malware, phishing attacks, and identity theft, and explains the importance of securing personal, organizational, and government data. The chapter emphasizes common security measures, including encryption, firewalls, antivirus software, and the use of strong passwords, to protect sensitive information from cyber threats. The chapter also explores cyber laws, which are designed to address legal issues related to the use of computers, the internet, and digital information. It introduces students to various cybercrimes, such as hacking, cyberbullying, data theft, and online fraud, and outlines how these crimes are dealt with under the law. The chapter focuses on key laws and regulations that govern internet usage, such as the Information Technology Act, which provides the legal framework for handling cybercrimes and electronic transactions in India. It also explains intellectual property rights in the digital age, including laws related to copyright, patents, and trademarks in the context of digital content. The chapter aims to equip students with a basic understanding of how to protect their personal data and how the legal system addresses the challenges posed by the increasing reliance on digital technology. By the end of the chapter, students are expected to understand the importance of information security and the role of cyber laws in maintaining a safe and lawful online environment.

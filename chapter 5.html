Chapter-5: Programming concepts and Logics

Chapter 5 typically focuses on "Introduction to Programming" or "Problem Solving using Algorithms" in Computer Science. It introduces the concept of algorithms, which are step-by-step procedures or formulas for solving problems. The chapter covers how to break down complex problems into smaller, manageable tasks and represent them as algorithms. It also discusses the importance of flowcharts and pseudocode as tools to design and visualize algorithms before actually writing code. The chapter may introduce basic programming concepts such as variables, data types, operators, and control structures like loops (for, while) and conditional statements (if, else). The focus is on building a strong foundation for students to understand how to write programs by thinking logically and solving problems systematically. Through examples and exercises, the chapter helps students develop problem-solving skills and learn how to express solutions in the form of algorithms and code.

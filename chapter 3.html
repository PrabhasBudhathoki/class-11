Chapter-3:Computer system and operating software

The chapter introduces essential concepts related to data representation in computers. It explains how computers use binary numbers (0s and 1s) to process and store information. Key topics include various number systems such as decimal, binary, octal, and hexadecimal, and how to convert between them. The chapter also covers how different types of data, like numbers, text, and images, are represented in memory. Special focus is given to character encoding systems like ASCII and Unicode, which allow computers to handle text. Finally, it touches on the basics of Boolean logic, which plays a critical role in computer processing and decision-making.
